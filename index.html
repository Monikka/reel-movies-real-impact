Reel Movies, Real Impact

Hollywood - Genres, Budgets, Profits, and everything else.


by Monikka Ravichandran

Do movies matter?

Movies allow us to escape realism. They let us dive into fictional worlds, take us places we have never been, broaden our perspectives and open our eyes to a wider world. It is almost as if there has been a spell cast that keeps our eyes glued to the screen and lets our mind wander into newer worlds. Every time I see a movie, while I watch them in awe, I can’t prevent myself from wondering if the time and money that went into entertaining us for a little while is worth the effort. But what is it that makes movies more appealing to people than any other form of art? It is as if there is magic that keeps our eyes glued to the screen.

Despite being a time-consuming tedious process that involves hundreds of people and thousands of dollars, the industry has only been growing steadily with no signs of slowing down in the coming years. In recent times, there has been sleuth of movies being released that not only captivate the audience but make a fortune at the box office. The latest trend in Hollywood seems to make fewer such films with large amounts of money invested in them. In this article, I take a deeper look at the movies made so far, the genre that appeals to the audience the most, revenue from films in recent times, the production studios that make the most from making movies, whether movies can influence real life. Let’s look at the number of movies released in Hollywood from the beginning to recent times.

A look at the Numbers

Running down the numbers, we take a closer look at Hollywood's evolution over the years. Since the rise of the “blockbuster" Hollywood has trended towards producing a small number of movies that are extremely costly rather than many movies with smaller budgets.

Hollywood saw an increase in the number of movies being released in the 90s but that trend has been steadily dropping in the recent years. Now, we see that though fewer movies are being made, the budget for these movies is HUGE. The average budget for a movie these days is around 100 million.

Below, I have listed the most expensive movies made until 2018 (budget > 200M). Looking at the numbers it is clear that most movies did well at the box office and earned several times their budget as profit for the production houses.

All but one (Titanic) of the most expensive movies were produced after the year 2000. Further still, 50 of the 59 were produced in the last ten years. The majority of the most expensive films are also part of a film franchise. Looking at the most expensive list of movies, it is interesting that most of the movies listed fall under one of the three category of films: Superhero, Fantasy and Animated.

The Horror

While movies requiring CGI tend to have high production costs, I wonder if this is always the case? Is Hollywood success formula "Spend more, Earn more"? Do only high budget movies turn out to make the most profits? Are there films of other genres that were profitable despite their low budgets?

I hoped not and then there was "Horror"!

Horror and psycho-thriller movies can be made without the need for too much CGI and therefore, costs less in production. But they compete on the same scale of the high budget movies.
