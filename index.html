Reel Movies, Real Impact

by Monikka Ravichandran

Do movies matter?

Movies allow us to escape realism. They let us dive into fictional worlds, take us places we have never been, broaden our perspectives and open our eyes to a wider world. It is almost as if there has been a spell cast that keeps our eyes glued to the screen and lets our mind wander into newer worlds. Every time I see a movie, while I watch them in awe, I can’t prevent myself from wondering if the time and money that went into entertaining us for a little while is worth the effort. But what is it that makes movies more appealing to people than any other form of art? It is as if there is magic that keeps our eyes glued to the screen.

Despite being a time-consuming tedious process that involves hundreds of people and thousands of dollars, the industry has only been growing steadily with no signs of slowing down in the coming years. In recent times, there has been sleuth of movies being released that not only captivate the audience but make a fortune at the box office. The latest trend in Hollywood seems to make fewer such films with large amounts of money invested in them. In this article, I take a deeper look at the movies made so far, the genre that appeals to the audience the most, revenue from films in recent times, the production studios that make the most from making movies, whether movies can influence real life.
